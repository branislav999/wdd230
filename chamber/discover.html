Demographics

  • Population: Approximately 1.23 million
  • Ethnic Groups: Serbs (86.6%), Romani (3.3%), Others (10.1%)
  • Languages: Serbian
  • Religions: Eastern Orthodox (73.9%), Roman Catholic (11.1%), Muslim (3.1%), Others (12.9%)
  • Median Age: 42.3 years
  • Population Density: Over 6,000 people per square kilometer

Events

  • Belgrade Music Festival (BEMUS): International music festival showcasing classical music performances.
  • Belgrade Boat Carnival: Colorful event taking place on the Sava River with decorated boats, music, and fireworks.
  • Belgrade International Film Festival (FEST): One of the most prominent film festivals in the region, featuring a wide range of international films.
  • Belgrade Dance Festival: Celebration of contemporary dance with performances by renowned dancers and choreographers.

Attractions

  • Kalemegdan Fortress: Historical fortress overlooking the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers.
  • Skadarlija: Cobblestone street known for its bohemian atmosphere, cafes, and traditional Serbian restaurants.
  • St. Sava Temple: One of the largest Orthodox churches in the world, known for its grand architecture and stunning interior.
  • Nikola Tesla Museum: Museum dedicated to the life and work of Nikola Tesla, featuring his inventions and artifacts.
  • Ada Ciganlija: Recreational area on an island in the Sava River, offering beaches, sports facilities, and cycling paths.
